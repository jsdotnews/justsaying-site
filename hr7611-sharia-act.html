What it does

The bill, explained

No spin. Here's what the legislative text actually says and the context around it.

📋

The core provision

Prohibits federal funds from going to any state or local government that bans residents from owning dogs or considers dog ownership forbidden ("haram") under religious law.

⚖️

Sense of Congress

Includes a nonbinding statement that "Sharia law is a foreign concept that is against the pursuit of happiness" and that "it is a right of all Americans to own a dog."

👥

Co-sponsors

Reps. Brandon Gill (R-TX), Chip Roy (R-TX), Mary Miller (R-IL), and Keith Self (R-TX). Roy and Self co-chair the Sharia Free America Caucus.

🏛️

Committee assignment

Referred to the House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform, chaired by Rep. James Comer (R-KY). No hearings scheduled.

How we got here

The bill followed a social media exchange between Rep. Fine and pro-Palestinian activist Nerdeen Kiswani, a supporter of NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani. Kiswani posted that "dogs definitely have a place in society, just not as indoor pets" and that "NYC is coming to Islam." She later said the post was satirical, referencing melting snow revealing dog waste.

Rep. Fine responded that "the choice between dogs and Muslims is not a difficult one," which drew bipartisan criticism and calls for censure.

ℹ️ Worth noting: No U.S. jurisdiction currently prohibits dog ownership. Some municipalities have breed-specific restrictions, but those are unrelated to religious law. This bill addresses a scenario that does not appear to exist in current law.

FAQ

Common questions

Does any U.S. jurisdiction ban dog ownership?

No. No U.S. jurisdiction prohibits residents from owning dogs. Some municipalities have breed-specific laws restricting certain breeds, but those are unrelated to religious law.

What happens if the bill stays in committee?

Most bills introduced in Congress never leave committee. If no hearings or markup sessions are scheduled, the bill dies at the end of the 119th Congress in January 2027. It could be reintroduced in a future session.

Is this a bipartisan bill?

As introduced, the bill has only Republican co-sponsors. No Democratic members have signed on.
